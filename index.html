Introduction

PREVENT is one part of the government's overall counter-terrorism strategy: CONTEST. The aim of Prevent is to:

  • tackle the ideological causes of terrorism
  • intervene early to support people susceptible to radicalisation
  • enable people who have already engaged in terrorism to disengage and rehabilitate

Prevent duty training is for people working in sectors covered by the Prevent duty, such as:

  • education
  • health
  • local authorities
  • police
  • criminal justice agencies (prisons, probation, youth justice)

Risk factors

Early signs of susceptibility to radicalisation could be:

  • Sudden changes in beliefs or ideology, especially adopting extremist views
  • Withdrawal from family, friends, or usual activities
  • Increased secrecy, especially around online activities
  • Expressing hostility towards certain groups or authorities
  • Justifying the use of violence to support a cause
  • Seeking out and consuming extremist content online
  • Sudden interest in weapons or explosives

Compassion and Sensitivity

Many of these signs could also be related to other issues, and the presence of one or more doesn't necessarily indicate radicalization.
